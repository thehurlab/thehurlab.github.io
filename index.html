Research Highlights

Publication-based themes shaping the current prototype

The homepage focus areas below summarize recurring directions seen across the publication titles, from foundational work on Argonaute and piRNA biology to CRISPR engineering, mutation detection, and disease-oriented applications.
Theme 01

Genome Editing Technologies

CRISPR-Cas9 / Cas12a / Cpf1 Specificity and precision

The publication set supports a strong focus on precise genome editing, including single-base editing, target specificity, knock-in strategies, Cas12a nickase development, and genome-wide analyses of CRISPR nuclease behavior.

Theme 02

CRISPR-based Diagnostics and Mutation Detection

Rare mutation detection cfDNA and clinical samples

Several recent papers indicate an expanding diagnostic direction centered on low-frequency mutation detection, including MUTE-Seq and guide design strategies for sensitive detection of clinically relevant variants in circulating cell-free DNA.

Theme 03

RNA Biology and Gene Regulation

Argonaute / piRNA / TREX Transcriptional regulation

Earlier publications show a substantial foundation in RNA biology, including Argonaute regulation, piRNA-guided silencing, and TREX loading on nascent RNA. These titles support a broad emphasis on RNA-mediated gene regulation.

Theme 04

Disease Models and Translational Applications

Organoids and mouse models Gene and cell therapy relevance

The publication list also supports disease-oriented work spanning CRISPR-generated animal models, patient-derived cortical organoids, gene and cell therapy reviews, and clinically adjacent applications relevant to mutation analysis and therapeutic development.

Research Direction

A clean academic structure grounded in the publication record

This prototype now emphasizes thematic continuity rather than promotional claims. The content highlights what can be supported directly by the listed publications: CRISPR engineering, sensitive mutation detection, RNA regulation, and disease-relevant biological systems.

Where the titles suggest translational intent, the language remains broad. Specific clinical performance, institutional partnerships, recruitment details, and unverified awards have not been added to the summary sections.

Representative publication signals

  • MUTE-Seq and low-frequency mutation detection in cfDNA
  • Single-base and high-precision genome editing approaches
  • Cas12a, Cpf1, and guide engineering for specificity
  • Argonaute, piRNA, and TREX-related RNA regulation studies
  • CRISPR-generated disease models and gene/cell therapy reviews
