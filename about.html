Research Themes

Concise theme buckets derived from the publication list

Genome Editing Technologies

Multiple publications support a core interest in the design and evaluation of CRISPR editing systems, including precise single-base editing, Cas12a/Cpf1 engineering, specificity analysis, and strategies for efficient knock-in.

  • Precise and single-base genome editing concepts
  • Cas12a nickase and Cpf1-related engineering
  • Target specificity and guide design
  • Genome-wide assessment of nuclease behavior
Representative signals from titles 2021 review on precise single-base genome editing, 2022 Cas12a nickase work, 2020 chimeric DNA-RNA guide study, and 2016 Nature Biotechnology papers on Cpf1.

CRISPR-based Diagnostics and Mutation Detection

Recent publication titles indicate a diagnostic direction focused on detecting rare variants with high sensitivity, especially in circulating cell-free DNA and clinically relevant mutation contexts.

  • Low-frequency mutation detection
  • CRISPR-guided signal discrimination
  • cfDNA-oriented assay development
  • Single-base mutation readout strategies
Representative signals from titles MUTE-Seq in 2025 and the 2025 EGFR mutation detection paper support this theme directly.

RNA Biology and Gene Regulation

The earlier publication record shows a substantial foundation in RNA-mediated regulation, including Argonaute biology, piRNA-guided silencing, and TREX-related control of RNA processing and expression.

  • Argonaute protein regulation
  • piRNA pathway and transcriptional silencing
  • TREX loading and nascent RNA biology
  • Broad RNA-centered gene regulatory mechanisms
Representative signals from titles Publications from 2010, 2013, and 2016 support a broad RNA biology theme without requiring narrower claims beyond the titles.

Disease Modeling and Translational Applications

The publication set also supports a disease-facing research direction that connects CRISPR tools to model systems and therapeutic contexts, while remaining broader than any single disease program.

  • CRISPR-generated animal models
  • Patient-derived organoid systems
  • Gene and cell therapy-oriented reviews
  • Mutation analysis and disease-associated gene studies
Representative signals from titles The NF1 mouse model paper, progeria organoid paper, and review on gene and cell therapy applications support this translational bucket.
How to read this summary

Broad, disciplined interpretation

The site language has been revised to stay close to what publication titles can reasonably support. That means the theme descriptions emphasize methods, biological systems, and application areas that recur across the list, without adding unsupported detail.

For example, the publication record clearly supports CRISPR engineering, mutation detection, RNA regulation, and disease-related model systems. It does not justify detailed claims about specific clinical deployment, funding, or unpublished platform capabilities, so those claims are intentionally omitted.
